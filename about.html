About

I am an incoming Biostatistics Master's (capstone) student at the University of Washington. I received my Bachelor's degree in Mathematics from California Polytechnic State University, San Luis Obispo in March 2026. I have a strong interest in the intersection of quantitative methods, environmental systems, and health sciences, which I am looking to further study during my time as a student and in my future career.

At Cal Poly, I have completed rigorous mathematics coursework, and I want to apply the quantitative frameworks I have learned to understand complex natural systems that ultimately affect human health. My previous experiences at Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution and the University of Notre Dame Environmental Research Center have exposed me to mathemetical modeling in bioloigcal and ecological settings, and I am looking forward to exploring similar concepts in public health and health science research.
