Current and Past Projects

Cal Poly Senior Project:

Oxygen-Phytoplankton-Zooplankton Dynamics in a Spatial-Temporal PDE Model

Ocean oxygen concentration has a strong effect on the entire marine environment, and factors such as climate change, eutrophication, and upwelling have created more intense swings in oxygen content leading to extreme ecological consequences. It is important to model dissolved oxygen content so that there is a better framework to predict future oxygen concentration lows, and see the warning signs. Along with the dissolved oxygen concentration, this project aims to model phytoplankton and zooplankton concentrations in order to capture the dynamics between all three variables. A spatial component is added to the model to understand the spacial-temporal scales of oxygen minimum zones and a Fourier analysis is conducted.

Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution:

Plankton Population Dynamics

In a changing ocean, modeling the seasonal changes in cell size distributions is crucial to understanding the future health of our oceans. To have this predictive framework, a projective model that can estimate how increasing water temperatures will impact major phytoplankton groups like Synechococcus is necessary. Previous research has modeled cell size distributions of Synechococcus by projecting forward one day at a time, and then estimating growth, division, and loss rates. We build off these models to create a matrix projection model that captures daily and seasonal changes in cell size distributions. We add another transition state, respiration, and add temperature dependencies to the growth and division functions to improve the model and attain direct relationships between cellular processes and environmental conditions.

University of Notre Dame Environmental Research Center:

LiDAR-Based Mapping and the Parameterization of a Hydrological Model

This project entailed LiDAR-Based Mapping of Watersheds of the University's field station in a north temperate mixed deciduous forest environemnt. The calcualted elevation data along with large amounts of atmospheric forcing data was then used in parameterization of a hydrological model constructed in R to estimate lake water budgets.
